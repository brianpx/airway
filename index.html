Ambu vs. Verathon

A Strategic Comparison Based on Independent Scientific Evidence

At a Glance: Key Findings

Independent studies show a clear preference for Ambu's aScope 5 in overall performance and user satisfaction. However, the best choice depends on specific clinical priorities and existing hospital infrastructure.

1. Clinical Performance: The Clinician's Choice

In direct head-to-head studies, clinicians consistently rate Ambu's performance as superior.

  • A 2023 study found the Ambu aScope 5 was rated "excellent" (5.0/5.0), while the Verathon B-Flex was rated "unsatisfactory" (2.7/5.0) by interventional pulmonologists.[4]
  • A larger 2025 study with 31 physicians confirmed the Ambu aScope 5 HD was rated highest in both performance and sampling, while the Verathon B-Flex and B-Flex 2 ranked lowest among six scopes evaluated.[5]
Key Takeaway: Independent clinical data demonstrates Ambu's superior performance and physician acceptance, with performance now on par with or exceeding reusable scopes.[6]

2. Portfolio Breadth: Enterprise Platform vs. Niche Solution

Ambu provides a comprehensive platform for hospital-wide standardization, while Verathon focuses exclusively on airway management.

Specialty Ambu: The Enterprise Platform Verathon: The Airway Specialist

Strategic Value: Ambu enables standardization on a single, integrated platform, simplifying training, procurement, and workflow across the entire hospital system. Verathon offers a powerful, synergistic "Total Airway Solution" by integrating with its market-leading GlideScope video laryngoscopes.

3. Risk & Reliability: A Critical Safety Distinction

Analysis of the unbiased U.S. FDA MAUDE database reveals a significant differentiator in patient safety profiles.

Ambu: Favorable Risk Profile

  • No reports of catastrophic in-vivo mechanical failures found in regulatory data.[7]
  • Guaranteed sterility eliminates the risk of patient-to-patient cross-contamination.[1, 2]

⚠ Verathon: Critical Safety Concern

FDA MAUDE Report (Event Date: 06/17/2020): Documents a catastrophic in-vivo failure of a Verathon BFlex 5.0 bronchoscope.[8]

  • Event: The distal tip (camera module) detached inside a patient's airway.
  • Outcome: Required a second, unplanned endoscopic procedure to retrieve the broken component.[8]
Key Takeaway: The documented mechanical failure of the Verathon BFlex, sourced from an unbiased government regulatory database, introduces a significant patient safety risk.

4. Economic & Operational Value: Evidence from Systematic Reviews

Independent, peer-reviewed economic studies show that single-use platforms are highly cost-effective when the total cost of ownership is considered.

A Clear Financial Advantage: The Single-Use Model[10]

A systematic review found single-use bronchoscopes can save an estimated:

$157

per procedure compared to reusables when accounting for reprocessing and repair costs.

The Economic Imperative: Why Single-Use Outperforms Reusable[9]

Another systematic review calculated a 2.8% infection/contamination risk with reusable scopes. Factoring in treatment costs more than doubled the effective cost per procedure for reusables (~$649 USD vs. ~$279 USD for single-use).

Key Takeaway: The economic case for single-use endoscopy is validated by independent research. It reduces operational costs, mitigates financial risk from infections, and improves hospital workflow.

5. Cost Comparison: It's More Than Just the Scope

While per-scope prices are comparable, the total cost of implementation depends heavily on capital equipment for visualization.

Ambu: Enterprise Value

Requires an initial capital investment in Ambu's aView or aBox monitors. However, this single platform is scalable across Pulmonology, GI, Urology, and ENT, offering significant long-term savings through enterprise-wide standardization.

Verathon: Leveraging Existing Infrastructure

Offers a major cost advantage for hospitals that already have an installed base of GlideScope video laryngoscopes. The BFlex scopes integrate directly, minimizing or eliminating new capital expenditure for airway management procedures.

Which Bronchoscope is Right for You?

Choose Ambu if...

Your priority is overall user satisfaction, high-quality HD visualization, a highly-rated sampling system, or implementing a standardized single-use endoscopy platform across multiple hospital departments.

Consider Verathon if...

Your hospital has a large installed base of GlideScope monitors and your primary need is for airway management, or if you are prioritizing the lowest possible upfront capital investment.
